What is Kima Network?

Kima is a universal, decentralized settlement layer that enables transfers and payments between disconnected systems: public blockchains, private blockchains, and bank accounts.

What makes Kima's technology unique?

Kima represents a pioneering advancement in facilitating interoperability among disparate financial ecosystems. Its unique Cross-Blockchain Settlement Engine operates independently of Smart Contracts, enabling seamless and compliant transfers of digital and traditional currencies across various financial infrastructures. With its innovative and patented technology, Kima stands out in the market as a game-changer in bridging financial divides.

Is Kima Network a bridge?

No, Kima is not a bridge. As compared to crypto bridges, Kima takes a different approach. Kima does not use smart contracts, does not leverage oracles (utilized by smart contracts), and does not wrap tokens. Kima is an interoperability protocol that transfers value directly by managing pools of tokens on different blockchains, thus facilitating the transfer of value between them.

What's the utility of the $KIMA token within the ecosystem?

The $KIMA token plays a central role in our ecosystem, serving multiple purposes such as covering transaction fees, participating in network security through staking, and functioning as a governance token enabling holders to vote on crucial matters. Its utility is intricately crafted to expand alongside our platform, thereby driving up demand as adoption increases.

What is Kima's business model and how it generates revenue?

Kima's revenue strategy revolves around transaction fees for its seamless, secure cross-chain settlement services. We offer our SDK and API licensing to developers in both Web2 and Web3 environments and collaborate with financial institutions to facilitate trading tokenized Real-World Assets (RWAs). This diversified approach ensures a consistent revenue flow while fostering the ecosystem's growth.
